Objective Of Organic Farming

       

  1. To provide food of high nutritional quality in sufficient quantity.

 2. To work with natural systems rather than dominating them.

 3. To encourage and enhance the biological cycles within farming system involving micro-organisms, soil flora and fauna, plants and animals.

4. To maintain and increase the long term fertility of soils.

5. To avoid all forms of pollution that may result form agricultural techniques.

6. To maintain the genetic diversity of the agriculture system.

7. To consider the wider and ecological impact of the farming system.

 8. To maintain the ecological balance.
